## 1 — Market thesis

**Spend gravity (baseline):** The concept’s daypart ladder (day club–→ dinner–→ nightclub) aligns with **Fort Lauderdale Beach premium hotels**, **Las Olas / downtown hospitality**, and the **17th Street / airport–yacht corridor** — where affluent locals, boating wealth, inbound visitors, private aviation friction, parents-weekend spend, snowbirds/conference guests stack.

**Mega-event halo (Hard Rock Stadium):** [Hard Rock Stadium](https://www.hardrockstadium.com/events/) sits in **Miami Gardens (~347 Don Shula Dr)** — roughly **25–35 miles north of Broward nightlife**. Maps-agent guidance (variable traffic): **~30–45 minutes** Las Olas / downtown ↔ stadium. That is **rideshare/regional halo**, **not stadium-adjacency**. Favor playbook sites that monetize **weekend premium spend** locally; treat Dolphins / **FIFA World Cup 2026**, F1-associated weekends, Bruno Mars Sep 2026, Usher/R&B residency-style tours, stadium soccer as **traffic + hotel compression events** layered on Broward—not reason to relocate to northwest Miami‑Dade for real estate economics.

**Collegiate overlay:** **Florida Atlantic University** is academically relevant for student adjacency narratives, but the **economic engine** geography is Boca main campus—not Fort Lauderdale doorstep. Practical split: prioritize **metro-wide celebration spend** (+ optional student marketing overlays) versus **walking-distance campus tailgates** (not here).

**Confidence:** Drive-time band **medium confidence** (Google Maps Zo sub-agent summary); roster of 2026 Hard Rock marquee events **medium confidence** derived from stadium marketing pages (verify ticketing dates commercially before revenue modeling).
